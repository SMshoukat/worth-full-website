Frequently Asked Questions

1. Are there discounts on Thanksgiving?

Yes, there are often substantial discounts and special promotions offered by retailers on Thanksgiving, both in physical stores and online. These sales are often part of the larger holiday shopping season that includes Black Friday and Cyber Monday.

2. How much discount can you get on Thanksgiving Day?

The amount of discount you can get on Thanksgiving Day varies widely depending on the retailer, the specific products and the year. Some stores offer substantial discounts, particularly on big-ticket items like electronics, appliances, and furniture. Discounts can range from 10% to as high as 50% or more in certain cases.

3. Are there Black Friday deals after Thanksgiving?

Yes, Black Friday deals traditionally continue after Thanksgiving. While Black Friday itself is a major shopping event, many retailers extend their sales and promotions throughout the weekend and into Cyber Monday. This period, often referred to as the "Black Friday Weekend," is characterized by continued discounts and special offers.

4. Do Black Friday deals start on Thanksgiving?

Yes, Black Friday deals often start on Thanksgiving. Many retailers have adopted a practice known as "Grey Thursday" or "Thanksgiving Day Shopping," where they begin offering Black Friday discounts and promotions on the evening of Thanksgiving, sometimes even earlier in the day. This allows shoppers to access deals before the official start of Black Friday.

5. Are deals better on Thanksgiving or Black Friday?

On average, deals tend to be better on Black Friday compared to Thanksgiving. Based on previous years, the average discount on Thanksgiving Day is 20% off, and then rises to roughly 37% on both Thanksgiving and Black Friday.

On Thanksgiving, some stores may offer exclusive early access or "doorbuster" deals to incentivize shoppers to start their holiday shopping early. These can be highly attractive, but they might be limited in quantity or available for a short period of time.

Black Friday, on the other hand, is traditionally known for a wider range of discounts across various product categories. Retailers often roll out extensive promotions, both in-store and online, with the intention of attracting a large volume of shoppers. This can lead to a wider selection of deals compared to Thanksgiving.

With the evolving nature of retail and e-commerce, the distinction between Thanksgiving and Black Friday deals has become less clear, with some stores offering continuous promotions throughout the entire weekend. Ultimately, whether deals are better on Thanksgiving or Black Friday can depend on the specific retailer, the type of products you're interested in, and whether you prefer shopping in-store or online. It's a good idea to keep an eye on advertisements and promotions from your preferred stores to make sure you don't miss out on the best deals, regardless of whether they start on Thanksgiving or Black Friday.
